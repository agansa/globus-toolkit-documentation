Argonne plays key role in new Distributed Terascale Facility Project

The world's most powerful computational infrastructure is being built by NSF's Partnerships for Advanced Computational Infrastructure (PACI). Research institutions NCSA, SDSC, Argonne, and Caltech will work in conjunction with IBM, Intel Corporation, and Qwest Communications, Myricom, Sun Microsystems, and Oracle Corporation. Utilizing distributed systems based on Globus Grid technology, the partnership will set up and integrate new Linux clusters to supply a total of 11.6 teraflops of computing power in addition to the 2 existing clusters at NCSA, for the development of large scale data base applications.

Globus, the toolkit for programming grid computing systems and the basic software infrastructure for systems that integrate geographically distributed computational and information resources, along with a message-passing interface (MPICH-G2) and a science toolkit (CACTUS) will be used to coordinate computational activity across the 4 site clusters.
